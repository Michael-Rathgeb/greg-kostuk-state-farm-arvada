What state has the most people without health insurance?

Texas consistently has the highest number and rate of uninsured residents in the United States. According to U.S. Census Bureau data, roughly 16–18% of Texans lack health insurance, the highest uninsured rate of any state. Texas is one of the few remaining states that has not expanded Medicaid under the Affordable Care Act, which significantly contributes to its high uninsured population. Other states with notably high uninsured rates include Oklahoma, Georgia, Florida, and Wyoming.

Who has the lowest car insurance rates in Georgia?

State Farm and GEICO are frequently cited among the insurers offering the lowest car insurance rates in Georgia for many driver profiles. Rates vary significantly based on driving history, age, vehicle type, and location within the state. USAA often offers competitive rates but is limited to military members and their families. Georgia drivers typically pay above the national average for auto insurance, so comparing multiple carriers is essential to finding the most affordable coverage for individual circumstances.

Who has the lowest auto insurance rates in Ohio?

Erie Insurance, State Farm, and GEICO are consistently ranked among the most affordable auto insurers in Ohio across multiple rate comparison studies. Ohio has one of the lower average auto insurance costs in the country, making it a competitive market for drivers. The lowest rate for any individual depends on factors like age, driving record, coverage level, and ZIP code. Comparing quotes from several highly-rated insurers is the most reliable way to identify the best rate.

What is the 50% rule in insurance?

The 50% rule in insurance is a threshold used in some states where a party who is 50% or more at fault for an accident cannot recover damages from the other driver's liability policy. This falls under comparative negligence law. Colorado follows a modified comparative fault standard with a 50% bar, meaning an at-fault driver who is equally or more responsible than the other party loses the right to collect compensation. Is $500 a month a lot for insurance?

It depends. on what types of insurance are included in that total. For a single auto policy, $500/month is high. However, if that figure covers multiple policies — home, auto, life, and umbrella — it may be reasonable or even competitive. Factors like driving history, home value, coverage limits, and number of insured vehicles all influence total premiums. Comparing bundled policy costs against individual policies is one way to evaluate whether $500/month represents fair value for the coverage received.

Is $300 a lot for a car payment?

No. $300 per month is not considered a lot for a car payment by current standards. The national average monthly auto loan payment has risen above $700 for new vehicles and around $500 for used vehicles. A $300 payment typically reflects an older vehicle, a large down payment, or a shorter loan term. However, affordability depends on individual income — financial guidelines generally suggest keeping total vehicle costs, including insurance, below 15-20% of monthly take-home pay.
