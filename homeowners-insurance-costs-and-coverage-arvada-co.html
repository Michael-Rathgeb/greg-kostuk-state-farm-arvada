What is the 80% rule in homeowners insurance?

The 80% rule is the insurance industry standard requiring homeowners to carry coverage equal to at least 80% of their home's full replacement cost. If coverage falls below this threshold, insurers may only pay a partial claim rather than the full repair or rebuild amount. What is the 80/20 rule for home insurance?

The 80/20 rule in home insurance is another name for the 80% rule, referring to the requirement that dwelling coverage must equal at least 80% of the home's replacement cost value. This rule protects insurers from underinsurance and ensures claims are paid in full. Some insurers recommend carrying 100% replacement cost coverage to eliminate any risk of out-of-pocket shortfalls after a significant loss.
