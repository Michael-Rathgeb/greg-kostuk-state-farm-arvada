Which insurance company denies most claims?

No single insurer is definitively the worst for claim denials, as denial rates vary by claim type, policy terms, and region. What's the biggest mistake people often make when dealing with an insurance claim?

The biggest mistake is failing to document the damage thoroughly before making repairs or discarding property. Without photos, videos, and itemized records, it becomes difficult to substantiate the full extent of a loss. A second common error is giving recorded statements without fully understanding the policy terms involved. What happens if a claim goes 50/50?

When a claim is determined to be 50/50 fault — meaning both parties share equal responsibility — each driver's insurance typically covers their own damages under their respective policies. In states with comparative negligence laws, each party may recover only 50% of their total damages from the other's insurer. Colorado follows a modified comparative fault rule, meaning a party cannot recover damages if they are 50% or more at fault. This threshold makes accurate fault determination critically important in these situations. Learn more about general insurance rules explained.

What is the 50 percent rule?

The 50 percent rule in insurance law is a modified comparative negligence standard that bars a claimant from recovering damages if they are found 50% or more responsible for the accident. If a claimant is less than 50% at fault, they may recover damages reduced by their percentage of fault. This rule directly affects how auto insurance claims are evaluated and paid out in states like Colorado that follow this legal framework.
