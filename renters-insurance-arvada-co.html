What is the average cost of renters insurance in Colorado?

$15 to $30 per month is the typical range for renters insurance in Colorado, though annual costs can vary significantly. Key factors affecting price include the amount of personal property coverage selected, whether liability limits are standard or elevated, and the deductible chosen. In Arvada and the broader Denver metro area, local crime rates and weather risks like hail can also influence premiums. Bundling renters with auto insurance often reduces costs further.

What is a reasonable amount for renters insurance?

A reasonable amount for renters insurance is a policy that covers the full replacement value of personal belongings, typically $20,000 to $50,000, with at least $100,000 in liability protection. Most standard policies also include additional living expenses if a rental becomes uninhabitable. A $500 to $1,000 deductible is common. Renters with high-value items like electronics, jewelry, or musical instruments may need scheduled endorsements beyond the base policy to be fully protected.
